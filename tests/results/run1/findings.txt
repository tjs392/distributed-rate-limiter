PRESSURE-TIERED GOSSIP: PRELIMINARY BENCHMARK FINDINGS
Teiji Schoyen | Drexel University | March 2026
=======================================================

SETUP
-----
Cluster:       5 nodes, local machine (ThinkPad)
Rate limit:    1000 req / 60s window
Gossip base:   200ms interval
Strategies:    fixed, binary, 3tier, tiered (5tier), 8tier, continuous
Load levels:   75%, 100%, 200%, 300%, 400% of rate limit
Iterations:    5 per pressure level per strategy
Metric:        Over-admission ratio = (allowed - limit) / limit
               Convergence p75 = time for 3 of 4 observer nodes to reach
               80% of sent count after burst


MEAN OVER-ADMISSION RATIO BY STRATEGY
--------------------------------------
Strategy       200%     300%     400%
fixed          90.4%    96.2%    95.8%   <- baseline
binary         92.3%    87.1%   102.2%
3tier          80.7%    77.9%    76.7%
tiered (5t)    80.2%    94.7%    99.5%
8tier          62.5%    78.9%    64.3%   <- best overall
continuous     94.7%   100.1%    70.1%

All strategies: 0% over-admission at 75% and 100% load (below limit).
Convergence times at 300%+ are not meaningful (saturation regime,
all nodes hit limit from local decisions alone before gossip fires).


KEY FINDINGS
------------
1. 8tier is the best-performing strategy overall.
   At 200% load it reduces over-admission by ~31% vs fixed (62.5% vs 90.4%).
   At 400% load it reduces over-admission by ~33% vs fixed (64.3% vs 95.8%).
   This challenges the original prediction that 5 tiers would be the sweet
   spot -- tier count alone does not determine performance.

2. 3tier is the most stable strategy across load levels.
   OA ratio is 80.7% / 77.9% / 76.7% at 200/300/400% load -- low variance
   and consistent improvement over fixed at every level. Good
   cost/benefit tradeoff for practitioners who want predictability.

3. Binary confirms the thundering herd problem empirically.
   At 400% load, binary is WORSE than fixed (102.2% vs 95.8%). This is
   the Spotify threshold-crossing problem: many keys crossing the 80%
   boundary simultaneously creates a burst of fast-tier gossip that
   contends rather than helps. This is a publishable negative result.

4. The OA reduction curve is not monotonic.
   8tier > continuous > 3tier > tiered (5tier) > binary > fixed at 200%.
   More tiers does not simply mean better performance. Tier boundary
   placement matters as much as tier count.

5. Continuous underperforms at moderate overload.
   At 200% load continuous is 94.7% -- worse than fixed. The pressure^2
   curve with alpha=2.0 reacts too slowly at moderate pressure. At 400%
   it recovers to 70.1%. Alpha tuning or revised tier boundary mapping
   for the continuous-to-discrete approximation is needed.

6. Tiered (5tier) regresses at high load.
   5tier was the original implementation and performed well in early runs
   but shows 94.7% / 99.5% OA at 300/400% load -- worse than 3tier and
   8tier. High iteration variance suggests tier boundary oscillation under
   sustained burst. Needs a rerun with more iterations to confirm.


OPEN QUESTIONS
--------------
- Is the 5tier regression at 300-400% reproducible or run-to-run noise?
  Rerun with ITERATIONS=15 to confirm.

- Why does 8tier outperform continuous? The continuous tier mapping
  (pressure^2 * 7 -> tier index) produces much lower tier assignments
  at moderate pressure than 8tier's evenly-spaced boundaries. The
  comparison is not apples-to-apples. Fix the continuous mapping to
  match 8tier intervals before drawing conclusions.

- Does improvement scale with N? All results are at N=5 (local cluster).
  The over-admission bound O_max = R * T_convergence * (N-1)/N grows
  with N. The benefit of tiered gossip should be more pronounced at
  N=25, N=50, N=100. Cloud cluster runs needed to confirm.

- What is the bandwidth cost of each strategy? OA reduction is only
  half the story. 8tier gossips faster for more keys, which increases
  aggregate UDP traffic. Not yet measured.
